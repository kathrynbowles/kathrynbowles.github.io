News

All the things that are going on with us

March 2025 - We host the inaugural Stem Cell Models of Neurodegeneration symposium

Over 300 researchers descended on Edinburgh for a 3-day symposium, hosted by the UK DRI as part of the Cure-ND network, on all things stem cell/iPSC and how we can use them to model neurodegenerative diseases. Plenary speakers included Sonia Gandhi (Crick Institute), Mahmoud Maina (University of Sussex & Yobe State University) and Sergiu Pasca (Stanford University), along with a whole host of incredible speakers from around the world. Check out more HERE

January 2025 - We speak about mitochondria dysfunction in tauopathies on CurePSP's new podcast

We recently had a new project funded by CurePSP to investigate mitochondrial dysfunction in iPSC-organoid models of tauopathy. We discussed the project and other things iPSC-related on their new podcast, The Collective Mind. Listen to our episode, and other Collective Mind episodes HERE

December 2024 - New publication from the Bowles lab characterising MAPT S305 splicing mutation iPSC lines

We describe our new isogenic panel of MAPT S305 mutation iPSCs, which express high levels of 4R tau in vitro. We have developed a panel of 26 clones, derived from 4 parent lines, spanning 3 mutations (S305N, S305I and S305S), with control, heterozygous and homozygous mutations.

6th-8th November 2024 - Connectome

We all headed down to Harrogate for three days of all-things-DRI. Kathryn Newton was awarded an NC3Rs prize for her hard work leading organoid protocol bootcamps for researchers around the UK, and we all celebrated with a "Celestial" night out!

October 2024 - SCMND 2025 is announced!

In collaboration with Cure-ND, the UK DRI is hosting a brand new symposium on using Stem Cells to model Neurodegenerative diseases! Registration and abstract submission are open to everyone through SCMND.com - come see us in Edinburgh in 2025!

2nd September 2024 - Alex Fröhlich joins the lab!

Alex joins us to work on our Tauopathy Challenge Workshop project, focused on astrocyte pathology in PSP

1st September 2024 - Labby 2nd Birthday!

We celebrated a little early, but September marks 2 full years of the Bowles lab!

18th April 2024 - NoSPRIG podcast interview

We talk to Helga MacFarlane and Julie Jones about our Parkinson's UK funded work on LRRC37A2. Listen to it, and all other NoSPRIG podcasts HERE

25th-26th March 2024 - Tau 2024

The entire Bowles lab travelled to Washington DC to attend this year's Global Tau 2024, with a little side trip to NYC!

1st December 2023 - Dr. Dianne Lopez joins the lab!

Dr. Dianne Lopez joins us as the second postdoc in the Bowles lab. She will be working on our Parkinson's UK funded project, looking at the role of LRRC37A2 in astrocytes, and how it might protect against Parkinson's risk.

3rd-4th October 2023 - Connectome

We all headed down to Brighton for the UK DRI's annual conference. Our experience was complete with sessions around tau, genetics and EDI in the DRI, followed by Neon night!

1st September 2023 - Labby Birthday to us!

The Bowles lab celebrates their first year!

11th July 2023 - New pre-print on bioRxiv describing an optimized protocol for organoid differentiation and QC

We published a new pre-print, led by Dr. Taylor Bertucci in Dr. Sally Temple's lab, which describes their optimized method for the best QC steps and differentiation protocols for making reproducible and reliable forebrain organoids with high efficiency.

10th July 2023 - Lois Keavey joins the lab

Lois Keavey joins us as a Research Assistant, while she is finishing up her Master's thesis. Lois will be working on our Tau Consortium-funded project to investigate the effects of neuron-astrocyte interactions in iPSC models of tauopathy.

6th June 2023 - New pre-print introducing our MAPT S305 mutation iPSCs goes LIVE

We describe our new isogenic panel of MAPT S305 mutation iPSCs, which express high levels of 4R tau in vitro. We have developed a panel of 31 clones, derived from 4 parent lines, spanning 3 mutations (S305N, S305I and S305S), with control, heterozygous and homozygous mutations.

6th March 2023 - Dr. Filipa Henderson-Sousa joins the lab

Our first PostDoc joined the lab! A huge welcome to Dr. Filipa Henderson-Sousa! Filipa will be working on our iPSC-organoid models to understand the role of ELAVL4 in tauopathy synaptic dysfunction.

10th February 2023 - Kat is profiled by the UK DRI

Kat sat down with Molly Andrews at UK DRI headquarters to talk about starting up her lab and why tau is so great! In honour of International Women in Science Day, they also discuss the challenges and experiences of being women in academia

31st January 2023 - Kathryn Newton joins the lab

Today we welcome the very first Bowles lab member! Kathryn Newton joins us as our Senior Research Associate. Kathryn has extensive experience in culturing stem cells, and will be working to develop, optimise and validate organoid protocols.

December 2022 - Kat is Guest-Editing a special Tauopathy edition of Biomedicines

Kat is teaming up with Dr. Catarina Silva as co-guest editor for a special edition of Biomedicines; "Tauopathies: New Perspectives and Challenges." We are looking for both review articles and original research to highlight in this special issue. Please get in touch with either Kat, Dr. Silva or the Biomedicines editorial board if you would like to be a contributor.

1st September 2022 - The Bowles lab officially opens!

It starts today! The Bowles lab is officially established at the UK Dementia Research Institute at the University of Edinburgh!
