Get to know us!

Dr. Filipa Henderson-Sousa - Postdoctoral Researcher

fhender3@ed.ac.uk

Dr Filipa Henderson-Sousa joined the Bowles lab in 2023 to investigate the early molecular mechanisms underlying tauopathy using iPSC models. She completed her PhD in Dr Barlow’s lab at Edinburgh Napier University and then moved to the Netherlands to join Bogaert’s group as a postdoctoral fellow. She also delved into the world of industry where she worked on developing iPSC-derived cardiac cell organoids. She enjoys long-distance bike rides around Scotland in the summer (too cold in any other season), trail running (also a summer activity) and weight lifting (all-year-round activity). She also enjoys reading– anything and everything – and beach holidays, mainly in Portugal where she is originally from. Filipa is working on understanding the role of ELAVL4 expression on tauopathy pathogenesis in iPSC-organoids.

Kathryn Newton - Senior Research Technician

knewton@ed.ac.uk

Kathryn graduated from Aberdeen University with a BSc in Genetics and her first job was working for the Medical Research Council studying pattern formation in hind limbs. She moved on to a stem cell lab and has never looked back. She has mainly worked with stem cell differentiation protocols, histology, molecular biology and microscopy. She has continued to work as a Research Technician preferring to be playing in labs rather than stuck in an office writing grants and papers. She loves holidays in the sunshine mainly Madeira, swimming in the sea and snorkelling. She also enjoys cooking and tries to run twice a week to keep fit all year round weather permitting. In the lab, Kathryn is establishing and supporting iPSC and organoid differentiation protocols and QC across multiple projects.

Lois Keavey - Medical Research Scotland PhD Student

lkeavey@ed.ac.uk

Lois graduated from the University of Edinburgh with a BSc in Biomedical Sciences with honours in Anatomy and Development. She remained in Edinburgh to undertake an Masters by Research in Neuroscience in the Centre for Discovery Brain Sciences, investigating the spread of pathological tau in tauopathies. She joined the Bowles lab as a Research Assistant in 2023 and started her PhD in 2024. She enjoys music and crochet, and has recently started climbing. Lois has been working on our Rainwater funded project to investigate neuron-glia interactions in primary tauopathies and is now investigating disrupted ELAVL4 subcellular localisation.

Dr. Dianne Lopez - Postdoctoral Researcher

dlopez@ed.ac.uk

Dianne obtained her PhD in Biological Sciences at the University of Southampton under the supervision of Dr Katrin Deinhardt. Using cultured neurons, microscopy, luminescence assays and electrophysiological techniques, she investigated the molecular mechanisms of tau propagation in tauopathies. She joined the Bowles lab to investigate the role of a novel astrocytic LRRC37A2 gene in Parkinson's disease using iPSCs. She enjoys playing sports all-year-round (e.g., netball, volleyball and ultimate frisbee), hiking and wild swimming. Besides her love for sport and outdoor activities, she has a passion for dancing and in arts and crafts, recently discovering a new found love for landscape painting.

Dr. Alex Fröhlich - Postdoctoral Researcher

afroehli@ed.ac.uk

Alex graduated from the University of Applied Sciences Darmstadt with a BSc in Biotechnology and from the Technical University Darmstadt with a MSc in Biomolecular Engineering. He obtained his PhD in Neurobiology and Neurodegeneration at the University of Liverpool which included him undertaking his final year of research at the Perron Institute in Perth, Australia. During this time, he investigated the role of transposable elements in neurodegenerative diseases using a combination of wet lab and bioinformatic approaches. Alex joined the Bowles lab to investigate astrocyte dysfunction in primary supranuclear palsy and other tauopathies using iPSCs and human brain tissues. Outside of lab, Alex enjoys outdoor activities (e.g., football, running, hiking and skiing), travelling, as well as enjoying some time indoors, whether it’s catching the latest movie at the cinema, bowling or trying out new restaurants.

Dr. Paula Beltran-Lobo - Postdoctoral Researcher

pbeltran@ed.ac.uk

Dr. Paula Beltran-Lobo joined the Bowles Lab in 2025 to study the interactions between astrocytes and the vasculature in tauopathies funded by an Alzheimer’s Society Postdoctoral Research Fellowship together with the Diaz Castro lab at the University of Edinburgh – UK DRI. She obtained her PhD in Neuroscience from King’s College London (KCL) in 2022 under the supervision of Prof. Wendy Noble and Dr. Beatriz Perez-Nievas, where she investigated the role of astrocyte and microglial P2X7R-mediated inflammatory signalling in Alzheimer´s disease. She expanded her work during her postdoctoral research in Dr.  Maria Jimenez-Sanchez’ lab at KCL exploring astrocyte-neuronal interactions in tauopathies with a focus on extracellular chaperones as a crosstalk mechanism. Outside the lab, she enjoys painting, cat sitting, travelling and wandering around Edinburgh.

Dana Rakotondrasolo - Erasmus Intern

Dana graduated from the University of Limoges with a Bachelor's degree in Life Sciences, specializing in Cellular Biology and Physiology, and is currently pursuing a Master's degree in Health Engineering, with a specialization in Cellular Engineering, at the University of Poitiers. Last summer, she completed an internship at the Neuroscience and Mental Health Innovation Institute in Cardiff, Wales, where she worked on 16p11.2 copy number variations and neurodevelopmental disorders. She really enjoys listening to music - her tastes are very diverse, and include songs in several different languages. She also loves reading, watching comedy shows, and travelling.
